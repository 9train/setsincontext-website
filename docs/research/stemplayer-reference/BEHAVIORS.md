## Useful Behavior Patterns

- Keep the first screen very quiet and spacious so the hardware object feels important before the user reads deeper copy.
- Let the product ease into place with slow floating motion instead of fast looping animation.
- Use small uppercase microcopy and compact floating labels to make hardware callouts feel precise without becoming dense.
- Reveal sections on scroll with soft upward movement and staggered timing so the page feels paced rather than abrupt.
- Favor gentle hover lift, shadow growth, and small scale shifts over flashy movement or large color changes.
- Keep persistent navigation minimal, pill-shaped, and light enough that it does not overpower the product story.
- Present dense information later through accordions or expandable panels so the main journey stays consumer-facing.
- Maintain a warm, nearly monochrome field so shadows, glow, and object depth do more of the visual work than bright color.

## Safe Translation For The DDJ-FLX6 Site

- Apply the hardware-first pacing to the FLX6 board SVG, not to any Stem product imagery or layout clone.
- Use scroll reveals on major sections, cards, and headings to add rhythm while keeping the page readable for average DJs.
- Add a slow floating board motion, subtle pointer-responsive parallax, and soft pulse glows around active control zones.
- Keep the navigation sparse and friendly with product labels like `Visualizer`, `Teaching`, and `Streaming`.
- Push technical depth into an expandable troubleshooting drawer so the debugger feels available but secondary.
- Use the same warm background, rounded cards, and soft blur treatment already established in this DDJ design lab.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling float, reveal, pulse, and parallax behavior when motion reduction is requested.

## Do Not Copy

- Do not copy Stem code, assets, logos, typography stack choices, product images, product names, or page copy.
- Do not reproduce Stem's specific product grid, floating SKU arrangement, purchase controls, or branded player UI.
- Do not imply any relationship between the DDJ-FLX6 project and Stem beyond motion and pacing inspiration.
